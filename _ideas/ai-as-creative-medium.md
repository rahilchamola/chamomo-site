Most people talk about AI as a productivity tool — do the same thing faster. That frame misses the interesting part.

The better analogy is an instrument. A synthesizer doesn't just play piano sounds faster. It creates entirely new sounds that couldn't exist before. AI should be thought of the same way.

## The Instrument Frame

When you treat AI as an instrument, several things change:

**Practice matters.** You get better at prompting the same way you get better at playing guitar — through deliberate practice, not by reading tutorials.

**Style emerges.** Two people using the same AI will produce different outputs because they bring different creative instincts to the interaction.

**Collaboration > delegation.** The best outputs come from a back-and-forth creative process, not from handing off a task and waiting for results.

## Where This Leads

If AI is a creative medium, then the interesting question isn't "what can AI do?" but "what can I create with AI that I couldn't create without it?" That's a fundamentally different starting point.

I'm exploring this through my own work — using AI to build systems, create content, and remix ideas across domains. The results are distinctly mine, even though the medium is shared.
