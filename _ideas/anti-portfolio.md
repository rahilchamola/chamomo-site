Bessemer Venture Partners famously publishes their anti-portfolio — the companies they passed on (Apple, Google, etc.). It's honest, memorable, and reveals more about their thinking than their actual portfolio does.

What if individuals maintained the same thing? Not a resume of accomplishments, but a record of deliberate non-choices:

- "I chose not to pursue consulting recruiting because the learning curve was generic."
- "I turned down the club presidency because the time cost exceeded the signal value."
- "I didn't build the full SaaS version because the prototype taught me the market wasn't ready."

**Why this matters:** In a world that celebrates accomplishment, your *choices not to do things* reveal your values and strategy more clearly. An anti-portfolio is a strategic narrative told through omission.

**Implementation:** Add an `/anti` page to this site. Maintain a running list with brief explanations. Update quarterly.

The risk: this could come off as humble-bragging ("look at all these great things I *could* have done"). Need to frame it as genuine reflection, not performance.
