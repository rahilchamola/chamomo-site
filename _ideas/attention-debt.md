Technical debt is well understood: shortcuts in code that save time now but cost time later. I think there's an equivalent for attention.

Every app you install, every newsletter you subscribe to, every group chat you join — these are **attention debts**. They don't cost much individually, but they compound. And unlike financial debt, there's no credit score to warn you when you're overextended.

Most MBA students are attention-bankrupt by Term 2. Twelve WhatsApp groups, three email accounts, Slack for clubs, Instagram for social proof, LinkedIn for recruiting. Each one takes a tiny slice of attention. Together, they leave nothing for deep work.

**The attention audit:** List every recurring attention commitment. Assign each a weekly time cost (including context-switching overhead). Sum them up. Compare to the 112 waking hours per week. Most people are already overallocated before they start any actual work.

Need to develop this further — could be a useful framework for the ADHD-and-productivity piece.
