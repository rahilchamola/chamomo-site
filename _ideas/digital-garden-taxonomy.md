A way to think about the lifecycle of ideas on a personal site.

**Seed** — a fragment, a question, a hunch. Barely formed. Worth capturing but not worth polishing.

**Growing** — has a thesis, some supporting evidence, a direction. Worth sharing but still evolving.

**Mature** — a fully formed framework or argument. Polished enough to stand on its own.

The key insight: most ideas never need to be mature. Seeds and growing ideas are valuable because they show thinking-in-progress. The garden metaphor works because gardens are alive — things grow, change, and sometimes die.
