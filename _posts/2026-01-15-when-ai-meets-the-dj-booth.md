The first time I used an AI tool to help plan a set, it recommended four tracks in a row that were all in the same key. Technically correct. Musically dead.

That experience taught me something important about AI in creative workflows: **the tool doesn't understand vibe**. It understands structure. And that distinction matters.

## What I've been testing

Over the past three months, I've run every set prep through a combination of manual curation and AI-assisted analysis. The stack looks like this:

- **BPM Supreme** for the library and extended intros
- **Mixed In Key** for harmonic analysis
- **A custom GPT** I built that takes a venue description, crowd profile, and time slot — then suggests energy curves

The GPT doesn't pick songs. It picks *shapes*. "Start at 65% energy, dip at the 20-minute mark for a breather, peak at 45 minutes, sustain." That shape becomes my skeleton, and I fill it with tracks I know.

## Where AI actually helps

Three areas where AI genuinely saves me time:

**Crate organization.** I have 4,000+ tracks across genres. Tagging them manually by energy, mood, and mixability used to take hours. Now I batch-process metadata and get 80% accuracy on mood tags.

**Transition suggestions.** Given two tracks, the model suggests where to start the blend based on phrase structure. It's right about 60% of the time — which is enough to speed up prep.

**Set post-mortems.** After a gig, I feed in the setlist and any crowd notes. The AI helps me spot patterns: which transitions got the best response, where I lost momentum, what I should try next time.

## Where it falls flat

AI has no taste. It can't tell you that dropping a throwback Bollywood remix after a deep house build creates a specific kind of euphoria that only works at 1 AM at an ISB party. That's lived experience. That's reading the room.

The tools are accelerants, not replacements. The DJ who uses AI to skip the boring parts of prep so they can spend more time on creative decisions — that's the move.

## What's next

I'm building a small tool that connects my BPM Supreme crate to a set planner with energy curve visualization. Early prototype, but the idea is: drag tracks onto a timeline, see the energy flow in real-time, get suggestions for gaps.

More on that when it's not embarrassing to show.
