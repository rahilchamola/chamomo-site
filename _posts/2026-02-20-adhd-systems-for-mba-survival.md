ISB moves fast. Seven courses simultaneously, group projects overlapping, recruiting timelines that ignore your academic calendar. For someone with ADHD, this is either a nightmare or a playground. I've tried to make it the second one.

## The core problem

ADHD doesn't mean I can't focus. It means I can't *choose* what to focus on. Hyperfocus is a feature, not a bug — but it's uncontrollable. I can spend 6 hours deep in a DJ set prep workflow and forget I have a finance exam tomorrow.

The MBA environment amplifies this because everything feels urgent and interesting. The signal-to-noise ratio is terrible.

## Systems that actually work

**Batch processing everything.** I don't check email throughout the day. I don't context-switch between assignments. Monday mornings: all admin and email. Tuesday afternoons: all readings. DJ prep happens in one Sunday session. This reduces the activation energy of switching tasks, which is where ADHD loses the most time.

**External scaffolding.** My calendar isn't a suggestion — it's a constraint system. If it's not on the calendar with a specific time block, it doesn't exist. I use Things 3 with aggressive due dates, and I treat the due date as the "start thinking about this" date, not the actual deadline.

**Strategic incompleteness.** Not everything deserves 100%. I explicitly decide which assignments get 70% effort and which get my full attention. This sounds obvious but for ADHD brains, the default is either full obsession or complete avoidance. Consciously choosing "good enough" for specific tasks is a skill I had to learn.

**Environment design.** I study in exactly two locations. The library for deep work, a specific coffee shop for collaborative work. The context triggers focus mode without requiring willpower.

## What doesn't work

Willpower-based systems. "Just focus more" is like telling someone with bad eyesight to "just see better." Motivation-dependent systems fail. Environment-dependent systems work.

Todo lists without time blocks. A list of 20 things with no temporal structure is anxiety fuel, not a productivity tool.

## The MBA advantage

Here's the thing nobody tells you: ADHD is actually an asset in some MBA contexts. The ability to rapidly synthesize across domains, to make unexpected connections, to hyperfocus under deadline pressure — these are superpowers in case competitions and group brainstorms.

The trick is building systems that protect against the downsides while channeling the upsides. I'm still iterating.
