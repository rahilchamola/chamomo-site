## Context

This was a practice session that turned out well enough to record. The premise: imagine you're opening a techno night. Your job is to take the room from empty-and-cold to full-and-ready over 2 hours. You're not the headliner — you're the architect of anticipation.

## The Approach

**First hour:** Ambient techno, dub textures, minimal percussion. The kind of music that fills a room without demanding attention. 120-124 BPM.

**Bridge section (1:00-1:20):** Gradually introducing harder kicks, acid lines, and rhythmic complexity. 124-128 BPM.

**Final 50 minutes:** Driving minimal techno with relentless grooves. Not peak-time bangers — that's the headliner's job — but music with enough energy that the room is physically moving. 128-134 BPM.

## Technical Notes

All vinyl for the first hour (yes, actual records). Transitioned to digital for the second hour when I needed tighter BPM control for the gradual acceleration.

The key learning: warmup DJing is harder than peak-time DJing. You have to restrain yourself. The temptation to play your favorite tracks is immense, but the discipline of building slowly is what makes the headliner's job possible.

## Verdict

Happy with this one. The pacing feels right. If I ever get booked as an opener for a proper techno night, this is the blueprint.
