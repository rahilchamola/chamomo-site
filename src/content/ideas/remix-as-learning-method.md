The act of remixing — taking a concept from one domain and translating it to another — might be the most effective learning technique that nobody teaches.

When I explain product-market fit using DJ analogies, I understand PMF better than if I just read the textbook definition. The translation forces me to identify the *core principle* beneath the domain-specific language.

**Hypothesis:** Students who regularly translate concepts across domains retain and apply knowledge better than those who study within a single domain.

**Test this:** Pick one concept per week from MBA coursework. Write a 200-word explanation using only metaphors from music/DJing. See if recall improves.

**Counter-argument:** This could just be elaborative encoding, which is already well-documented. The "remix" framing might not add anything beyond what spaced repetition + elaboration already provides.

Still, the framing matters for motivation. "Remix your learnings" is more energizing than "practice elaborative encoding."
