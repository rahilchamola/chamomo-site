## Context

ISB's PGP program runs 7 courses simultaneously with overlapping deadlines. Standard study approaches don't work for ADHD brains under this kind of load.

## The System

A three-layer architecture:

**Capture layer** — everything goes into a Notion inbox. Class notes, readings, case highlights, random thoughts. Zero friction, zero organization at the point of capture.

**Processing layer** — weekly batch sessions where I organize captures into course-specific databases, tag them by concept, and create spaced repetition cards for key frameworks.

**Synthesis layer** — before exams, I run a synthesis protocol: pull all cards for a course, write one-page summaries connecting major concepts, identify likely exam questions based on what the professor emphasized.

## Results

Went from scattered panic-studying to a repeatable system that takes about 5 hours per week for all courses. Exam performance improved by roughly one grade band across the board.

## Key Insight

The system isn't about studying more — it's about reducing the activation energy of studying. Every decision about *what* to study and *how* is pre-made. When it's time to work, I just execute the next step.
